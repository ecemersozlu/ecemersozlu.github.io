About

Welcome! I am a PhD candidate in Political Science at the University of Pittsburgh. My research focuses on comparative politics and political behavior, mainly in Western Europe.

I study how moral convictions shape political judgment in consolidated democracies. My dissertation examines the values that underlie citizens' democratic attitudes, how these values interact with policy preferences and democratic commitments, and how political elites invoke democracy in response to far-right challenger parties. Across these projects, I am interested in the trade-offs that parties and voters face when the cordon sanitaire becomes politically salient.

My work bridges comparative politics, political psychology, and computational social science. I use quantitative methods, text-as-data, and survey experiments to study both party-level strategy and individual-level opinion formation, with a broader interest in how citizens form political attitudes amid rapidly changing and often conflicting political information environments.

Before beginning my PhD, I earned an MA in Political Science from Koç University in 2023.
